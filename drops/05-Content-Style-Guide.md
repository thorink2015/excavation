# Groundwork Solutions: Content Style Guide

Writing rules for every piece of copy on the site, in emails, on social, and in proposals. Keep this open while writing.

---

## 1. Voice in one line

Contractor-to-contractor. Direct. Confident without bragging. Plain English over jargon.

---

## 2. The four rules that fix 80% of bad copy

1. **Lead with the answer.** No preamble, no setup. The first sentence of every section says the thing.
2. **Numbers beat adjectives.** "We trench 200 ft of sewer line in a day" beats "fast and reliable service."
3. **Short sentences.** 20 words max. If a sentence runs long, split it.
4. **Cut filler.** If a sentence does not earn its spot, delete it.

---

## 3. Banned words

These signal AI or corporate slop. Don't use them.

| Banned | Use instead |
|---|---|
| delve | dig into, look at |
| leverage | use |
| robust | strong, reliable, proven |
| comprehensive solution | (delete; just say what you do) |
| seamless | clean, clean hand-off |
| elevate | improve, raise |
| unlock | get to, reach |
| navigate | (delete; use a real verb) |
| in today's fast-paced world | (delete entirely) |
| harness | use |
| foster | build, grow |
| paramount | most important, critical |
| plethora | a lot of, many |
| myriad | many |
| streamline | simplify, speed up |
| empower | (delete; usually filler) |
| cutting-edge | (delete; show, don't claim) |
| world-class | (delete; show, don't claim) |
| best-in-class | (delete) |
| game-changer | (delete) |
| solutions | services, work |
| utilize | use |
| commence | start |
| terminate | end |
| facilitate | help, run |
| optimize | tune, improve |

---

## 4. Banned punctuation

| Banned | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Em-dash | comma, period, colon, parentheses |
| Double hyphen (used as em-dash) | same as above |
| Excessive ellipses (...) | period |
| Exclamation marks (more than one per page) | period |
| Semicolons in casual copy | period (start a new sentence) |

## 5. Sentence patterns to use

### Pattern A: Subject + verb + specific outcome

> We dig foundations to plan, on time, and hand off a clean lot.

### Pattern B: What + for whom + result

> Trenching for utility contractors. Inspection-ready every time.

### Pattern C: Problem + how we fix it

> Subs ghost. Schedules slip. We show up the day we said we would.

### Pattern D: Numbers up front

> 24 hour quotes. 200 ft of trench per day. 8 services. One crew.

---

## 6. Words and phrases to actually use

Sound like a contractor, not a marketing agency.

**Job-site verbs:** dig, break, haul, grade, trench, clear, drop, set, hit grade, stake out, shoot the elevation, button up, hand off, button down, run lines, pull a permit, sign off

**Trade nouns:** crew, sub, GC, lot, pad, footing, stub-out, lateral, slab, pour, pad prep, cut and fill, compaction, stockpile, spoils, grade, cut, fill, spec, inspector, plan set, takeoff

**Honest hedges:** "most jobs," "in the field," "we usually," "depending on the site," "with normal access"

**Avoid:** "synergy," "ecosystem," "platform," "experience," "journey," "transformative," "enable," anything ending in -ization

## 7. Heading and page structure

### H1 rules

- One H1 per page
- 6 to 12 words
- Names what the page is about, often with the city or area
- Active voice
- No "Welcome to" openers

**Good:** Pool Excavation for Builders Who Need a Clean Dig
**Bad:** Welcome to Our Pool Excavation Services Page

### H2 rules

- Use as section labels
- Question format helps AEO ("How long does utility trenching take?")
- Sentence case
- 3 to 8 words

### Direct answer block (top of every section)

Open every H2 section with a 40 to 75 word direct answer paragraph. AI engines extract this. Customers skim it.

**Pattern:**
> [What it is]. [What's included]. [Who it's for]. [Specific number, location, or scope].

**Example:**
> Pool excavation runs $4,000 to $14,000 in the Sacramento area, depending on size, soil, and access. Most residential digs take 1 to 2 days. We dig to gunite, fiberglass, or plaster spec, set steps and benches, and haul or stockpile spoils. Tight-lot capable through 36 inch gates.

### Body copy rules

- Paragraphs: 2 to 3 sentences
- Sentences: 20 words max
- One idea per paragraph
- Specific over general (numbers, equipment names, neighborhoods)
- No long lists of adjectives ("fast, reliable, professional, efficient")

## 9. Specific copy types

### Hero copy

- H1 + 1 line subhead + 1 to 2 buttons
- Subhead: 15 to 25 words, says what they get and how fast
- No paragraph in the hero. Save it for the next section.

### Stat tile

- Big number (Big Shoulders Display 800)
- One line label (max 4 words)
- Numbers must be true (don't say "15+ years" if you started this year)

### Testimonial

- 1 to 2 sentences max
- Real name, company, city
- Specific quote, not "great team!" generic
- If we don't have testimonials yet, the placeholder copy is in the website copy doc. Don't fake quotes.

### Form labels and microcopy

- Label format: "First Name *", "Email *"
- Helper text: small, Slate color, only when needed
- Submit button: "Get Free Quote" (never "Submit")
- Success message: "Got it. We'll be in touch within 24 hours."
- Error message: "That didn't go through. Try again or call 530-559-8502."

### CTA buttons

| Context | Text |
|---|---|
| Primary CTA | Get a Free Quote |
| Service detail page | Request a Quote |
| Above form | Get Free Quote |
| Phone callout | Call 530-559-8502 |
| Section link | See All Services / See All Industries |
| Read more block | Read the full page |

### Meta descriptions

- 150 to 160 characters
- Lead with primary keyword + city
- Include phone number or specific differentiator
- End with a soft CTA

**Pattern:** [Service] in [City]. [What we do or who for]. [Differentiator]. [CTA].

**Example:** Pool excavation in Sacramento. Dig to gunite, fiberglass, or plaster spec. Tight-lot capable. Most quotes within 24 hours. Call 530-559-8502.

### Image alt text

- 8 to 15 words
- Describe what's in the photo and why it matters
- End with a period
- Front-load the important detail
- No "image of" or "picture of"

**Good:** Mini excavator backfilling a residential foundation in Loomis, California.
**Bad:** Image of an excavator working at a construction site

## 10. Honesty rules

We're a new brand. Don't fake longevity.

**Don't say:**
- "15+ years of experience" (unless verified)
- "Hundreds of satisfied customers"
- "Voted #1 in the area"
- "Industry-leading"

**Do say:**
- "Owner-operated"
- "Quotes in 24 hours"
- "Licensed CA contractor" (once license is verified)
- "Northern California, based in Rocklin"
- "Want to be one of our first reviews? Get a quote." (placeholder for the testimonial section until reviews come in)

When something needs verification, mark it `[VERIFY]` in drafts and don't ship until confirmed.

## 11. Voice across channels

The voice is the same. The format adapts.

### Website
Direct, structured, scannable. H1, H2, lists, short paragraphs.

### Cold email
Even shorter. 50 to 100 words. One ask. No formatting. No greeting fluff.

> Hi [name], saw [project / company]. We're a Sacramento site prep crew. Excavation, trenching, demo. We hit grade and leave a clean lot. Free quote in 24 hours if you have a build coming up. Worth a look?
> Dardi
> 530-559-8502

### Social (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn)
Show the work. One photo, one line of caption, one tag.

> 80 ft trench for sewer lateral, Granite Bay. Inspector signed it the same morning.
> #excavation #sacramento

### Google Business Profile posts
Same as social. Photo + 2 sentences + CTA.

## 12. Editing checklist (run before publishing any copy)

- [ ] Read it aloud. If you stumble, rewrite.
- [ ] No banned words from section 3
- [ ] No em-dashes or double hyphens
- [ ] No sentence over 20 words
- [ ] No paragraph over 3 sentences
- [ ] Numbers and specifics, not adjectives
- [ ] Active voice (not passive)
- [ ] One H1 only
- [ ] H2s open with a direct answer
- [ ] CTA text matches the locked patterns in section 9
- [ ] No fake claims (revenue, years, customers we don't have)
- [ ] All `[VERIFY]` placeholders resolved or flagged
- [ ] Phone number formatted: 530-559-8502
- [ ] Email is the final brand domain (not a placeholder)
