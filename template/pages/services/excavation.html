When you need it

Excavation is the first scope on most new builds. You need a dig when you're putting in:

  • A new house foundation, basement, or crawl space
  • An ADU, garage, or out-building pad
  • A swimming pool or spa
  • A commercial slab or footing system
  • A septic tank, leach field, or rainwater system

If your project sits on rock, in clay, or on a sloped lot, get us out for a site walk before you finalize the budget. A bad dig assumption upstream blows up the whole schedule.

How we quote

Most jobs we can quote off the plans plus a 15-minute site walk. We give you an itemized number with separate lines for:

  1. Mobilization and demobilization
  2. Excavation by cubic yard at spec depth
  3. Haul-off (or fill if you need it brought back)
  4. Backfill and compaction after the concrete crew
  5. Contingency for unknowns: rock, water, unmarked utilities

If conditions change underground, we stop and call. We don't quietly run up the change orders.

Equipment

We size the machine to the lot, not the other way around. Most residential digs run on mini or compact excavators that fit through a 6-foot side yard. Bigger lots get bigger iron. Skid steers handle the spoils.

Pricing transparency

Excavation pricing in <%= it.city %> varies more than most trades. The biggest swings come from:

  • Soil conditions: rocky or clay-heavy soil takes longer and dulls more teeth.
  • Access: a tight side-yard adds time and limits machine choice.
  • Haul distance: dump fees and trucking add up if the dump site is far.
  • Depth: deeper digs need shoring or benching for safety.

We'll give you a real ballpark on the first call. The detailed itemization comes after we've seen the site.
