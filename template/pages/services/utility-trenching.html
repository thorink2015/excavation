How we run a trench

Trenching is unforgiving. Two inches too shallow and the inspector fails you. Bad bedding and your line cracks the first time the ground moves. We don't shortcut any of the following:

  1. Locate first. 811 / USA tickets in 72 hours before we break ground. We hand-dig anything near a marked line.
  2. Trench to spec. Depth depends on what's in the trench. Water below frost line. Sewer at the slope your engineer drew. Gas, electric, and data per your local AHJ.
  3. Bed and bury properly. Sand or pea gravel beneath and around the line. No native fill against the pipe.
  4. Tracer wire and warning tape. On every non-metallic line. So the next person who digs there finds it before they hit it.
  5. Compaction in lifts. Settling cracks pavement and breaks lines. We compact every 6 to 8 inches.

Permits and inspections

Most utility trenches need a city or county permit and an open-trench inspection before backfill. We coordinate with the inspector. If you're doing the permit yourself, we'll tell you what to ask for.

Coordinating with other trades

Trenches usually run between the plumbing rough-in, electric rough-in, and the concrete pour. We sequence around your other subs. If you're general-contracting your own build, we'll walk you through what needs to be where before our crew shows up.

Restoration after backfill

We don't do the asphalt or sod patch ourselves. But we leave the trench compacted and graded to within an inch of finish so your paver or landscaper can drop right in. If you need patch work coordinated, we have crews we trust and can quote it for you.
